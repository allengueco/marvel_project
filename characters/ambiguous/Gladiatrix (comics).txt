She gained her current enhanced strength through an augmentation given by Power Broker INC. Shortly afterward she abandoned her life as a wrestler and became part of the Grapplers.\n\nThey were forced to restrain [[Titania (Grapplers)|Titania]], who had just lost a battle from Battleaxe. When the Grapplers came face to face with the Thing, Gladiatrix decided to remain on the good side and helped him out, saving the day.<ref>\'\'Thing\'\' #33</ref> Later, Gladiatrix tried out for membership in [[Avengers (comics)|the Avengers]], but was denied after she almost got into a battle with [[Ursa Major (comics)|Ursa Major]].<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #352</ref>\n\nShe agreed to join Superia\'s [[Femizons]] afterward, becoming a supervillain once more. She was attacked by [[Captain America]] and [[Paladin (comics)|Paladin]], whom Gladiatrix manages to capture. The heroes eventually rescued themselves and defeated Gladiatrix afterwards.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #387-392</ref> She would later be seen attending the  [[Advanced Idea Mechanics|A.I.M.]] Weapons Expo on an island protected by international law.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #411-414</ref>\n\n===Civil War===\n{{Main|Civil War (comics)}}\nHowever, during the Civil War event, Gladiatrix chose the side of the heroes once more and became involved with a gang that was strongly against the Super-Hero Registration Act. Her allies include, but are not limited to, [[Solo (Marvel Comics)|Solo]], [[Typeface (comics)|Typeface]] and [[Battlestar (comics)|Battlestar]]. She is apprehended when a [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] squad enters their hideout. She almost escapes, being responsible for the visiting journalist [[Sally Floyd (comics)|Sally Floyd]]. Debris knocks her down and Sally escapes on her own.<ref>\'\'Civil War: Front Line\'\' #4</ref>  She is later seen in the [[Negative Zone]] Prison, being contained in some virtual reality apparatus, begging for someone to help her.<ref>\'\'Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #535</ref> She, along with the other captive heroes were freed by [[Hulkling]].<ref>\'\'Civil War\'\' #6</ref> The people he freed assisted Captain America in their fight against the Pro-Registration forces. She did not accept the offer of amnesty.<ref name="civilwarbattledamage">{{Comic book reference | Writer = [[Anthony Flamini]] & [[Ronald Byrd]] | Penciller =  [[Scott Kolins]] | Inker = [[Scott Kolins]] | Story =  | Title = Civil War: Battle Damage Report | Issue = 1 | Date = March 2007 | Publisher = [[Marvel Comics]] | }}</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nGladiatrix has a degree of super-human strength, durability and stamina.
