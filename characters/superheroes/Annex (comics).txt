With powers resulting from [[prosthetics]] through computer technology, he possesses the ability to increase his physical abilities, as well as create any weapon he requires.\n\n==Publication history==\nAnnex was created by writer [[Jack C. Harris]] and artist [[Tom Lyle]] and first appeared in \'\'[[The Amazing Spider-Man|The Amazing Spider-Man Annual]]\'\' #27 (1993)<ref>{{cite book|last = Cowsill|first = Alan|last2= Gilbert|first2= Laura, ed.|chapter= 1990s|title = Spider-Man Chronicle Celebrating 50 Years of Web-Slinging|publisher = [[Dorling Kindersley]]|year = 2012|page = 203|isbn = 978-0756692360|quote= The debut story of Annex was written by Jack C. His next appearance was \'\'[[Spider-Man Unlimited]]\'\' #3, followed by a self-titled [[Limited series (comics)|limited series]], \'\'Annex\'\'.\n\nHe appeared in the \'\'[[Avengers: the Initiative]]\'\' series beginning with issue #13.\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nEllis is a [[Gulf War|Desert Storm]] veteran whose leg was wounded in combat.  The damage to his leg was so extensive, his leg had to be amputated.  He signed up for an Annexing unit, where [[computer technology]] will grow a newly functioning limb. Because of a [[computer glitch]], Ellis loses his memory and becomes the villain Annex. Annex is quickly defeated by Spider-Man, and changes back into Ellis.<ref>\'\'[[The Amazing Spider-Man]] Annual\'\' #27 (1993)</ref>\n\nAnnex joins Camp Hammond to be trained in the [[Fifty State Initiative]] program.<ref>\'\'Avengers: The Initiative\'\' #13</ref> After the [[Secret Invasion|Skrull invasion]], Annex was assigned to New Mexico\'s Initiative team.<ref>\'\'Avengers: The Initiative\'\' #21</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nAs a result of his exo-skeleton armor, Annex possesses the ability to increase his strength, speed, stamina, reflexes, reaction time, coordination, agility, dexterity, balance, and endurance. Annex can also create any weapon he requires.
