b"{{refimprove|date=April 2017}}\n\n{{Infobox comics character| <!--Wikipedia:WikiProject Comics-->\nimage=<!-- Deleted image removed: [[Image:DefensorHV.jpg|250px]] -->\n|caption=\n|character_name=Defensor\n|real_name=Gabriel Carlos Dantes Sepulveda\n|species=\n|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]\n|debut=''[[Marvel Super Hero Contest of Champions]] #1'' (Jun 1982)\n|creators=[[Mark Gruenwald]], [[Bill Mantlo]], [[Steven Grant]], and [[John Romita, Jr.]]\n|alliances=\n|aliases=\n|powers=\n|}}\n\n'''Defensor''' is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].\n\n==Publication history==\nDefensor first appeared in ''[[Marvel Super Hero Contest of Champions]]'' #1 (June 1982). He also appeared in issues #2-3 of the series (July\xe2\x80\x93August 1982).\n\nThe character subsequently appeared in ''[[The Incredible Hulk (comic book)|The Incredible Hulk]]'' Vol. 2 #279 (January 1983), and his death was revealed in ''Captain America'' #422 (December 1993).\n\nDefensor received an entry in the original ''[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe]]'' #3.\n\n==Fictional character biography==\n'''Gabriel Carlos Dantes Sepulveda''' was born in [[C\xc3\xb3rdoba, Argentina]].  He was a construction worker operating an excavating machine digging the foundation for an apartment building in C\xc3\xb3rdoba, when he unearthed the entrance to a hidden underground passageway.  He later sneaked back to the construction site at night and began to explore the labyrinth of catacombs beneath.  In one of the chambers he found a suit of armor resembling that of the Spanish conquistadores of the Sixteenth Century.  He decided to use this [[vibranium]] armor to be a champion of the people.\n\nLater he was teleported away by the [[Grandmaster (Marvel Comics)|Grandmaster]], along with hundreds of other heroes of Earth, so that the Grandmaster and [[Death (Marvel Comics)|Death]] could choose champions from among them. Defensor was chosen for the Grandmaster's team, fighting alongside fellow heroes [[Captain America]], the aborigine [[Talisman (Marvel Comics)|Talisman]] III, [[Darkstar (comics)|Darkstar]], [[Captain Britain]], [[Wolverine (character)|Wolverine]], [[Sasquatch (comics)|Sasquatch]], [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]], [[Peregrine (comics)|Peregrine]], [[She-Hulk]], [[Thing (comics)|Thing]], and [[Blitzkrieg (Marvel Comics)|Blitzkrieg]].  When the Grandmaster's team won the contest, the heroes were returned to Earth.<ref>''Contest of Champions'' #1-3</ref>\n\nThe '''[[Everyman (Marvel Comics)|Everyman]],''' posing as '''Zeitgeist''', later assassinated a number of South American superheroes, including Defensor.<ref>''Captain America'' #422</ref>\n\n==References==\n{{reflist}}\n\n==External links==\n*{{Marvunapp|http://www.marvunapp.com/Appendix/defensor.htm|Defensor}}\n*http://www.marveldatabase.com/Defensor\n\n[[Category:Fictional Argentine people]]\n[[Category:Marvel Comics superheroes]]\n\n\n{{Marvel-hero-stub}}"
