She first appeared in ''[[Marvel Fanfare]] vol. 1'' #12 (1984) and was created by [[Ralph Macchio (comics)|Ralph Macchio]] and [[George P\xc3\xa9rez]].\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nA skilled martial artist, Snapdragon was hired to defeat the [[Black Widow (Natalia Romanova)|Black Widow]] in [[Japan]].{{issue|date=December 2012}} She succeeded on her mission, but Black Widow soon regained her composure and quickly dispatched Snapdragon.{{issue|date=December 2012}} Later, Snapdragon joined [[Superia]]'s [[Femizons]] and became one of her lieutenants.{{issue|date=December 2012}} There, she found long-time enemy [[Diamondback (Rachel Leighton)|Diamondback]].{{issue|date=December 2012}} The two were trained by [[Anaconda (comics)|Anaconda]] in [[Taskmaster (comics)|Taskmaster]]'s Academy,{{issue|date=December 2012}} but Diamondback had subsequently dishonored Snapdragon in some form.{{issue|date=December 2012}} While on the S.S. ''Superia'' cruise ship, Snapdragon apprehended Diamondback and threw her off, leaving her to drown.{{issue|date=December 2012}} Diamondback recovered and received the super-soldier serum in one of her misadventures, and during their rematch, choked Snapdragon to death.{{issue|date=December 2012}} Her body was returned to Japan and cremated by her brother, Kono.<ref>''Captain America'' #413</ref>\n\nLater, the name Snapdragon was taken by [[Diamondback (Rachel Leighton)|Rachael Leighton]] after she began working for [[Superia]] as payment for finding a cure for the glitch in the super-soldier serum.<ref>''Avengers'' #387</ref>\n\nA woman calling herself Sheoke Sanada appeared in ''Moon Knight''.<ref>''Moon Knight'' Vol. 6 #2 (2011). Marvel Comics.</ref> If this is the original Snapdragon, details of her resurrection are unknown.\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nSnapdragon possesses no superhuman abilities other than a protective suit and a helmet with infrared sensors.
