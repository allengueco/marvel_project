b"{{Infobox comics character\n|image          = ChimeraX-Men.jpg\n|converted      = y\n|caption        = Chimera from ''[[Uncanny X-Men]]'' #503<br />Art by [[Greg Land]]\n|character_name = Chimera\n|real_name      =\n|species        = Extradimensional [[Mutate (comics)|Mutate]]\n|publisher      = [[Marvel Comics]]\n|debut          = ''[[Wolverine (comic book)|Wolverine]]'' vol. 2 #97 (January 1996)\n|creators       = [[Larry Hama]]<br>[[Adam Kubert]]\n|alliances      = [[Brotherhood of Mutants|Sisterhood of Mutants]]<br/>[[Marauders (comics)|Marauders]]\n|partners       = [[Dirtnap (comics)|Dirtnap]]<br/>[[Emplate]]\n|aliases        =\n|powers         = Generate telekinetic dragon-shaped flares<br>Telepathy\n|cat            = super\n|subcat         = Marvel Comics\n|villain        = y\n|sortkey        = Chimera\n}}\n'''Chimera''' is a fictional [[Mutant (comics)|mutant]] character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].\n\n==Publication history==\nHer first appearance was in ''[[Wolverine (comic book)|Wolverine]]'' vol. 2 #97 where she was created by [[Larry Hama]] and [[Adam Kubert]].\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nChimera is an interdimensional pirate from an unknown Earth who first encountered [[Wolverine (character)|Wolverine]] whilst she was gaining information on him and his [[feral]] regressive state. She assisted the self-styled heir of [[Apocalypse (comics)|Apocalypse]], [[Genesis (Marvel comics)|Genesis]], with Wolverine's capture when Genesis wanted to [[brainwashing|brainwash]] Wolverine to be his first [[Horsemen of Apocalypse|Horseman]]. They attempted to re-bond the [[adamantium]] to Wolverine's skeleton which had been removed by [[Magneto (comics)|Magneto]] but the process failed. Wolverine's body violently expelled the metal, killing most of Genesis' followers, the [[Dark Riders (comics)|Dark Riders]]. [[Dirtnap (comics)|Dirtnap]]\xe2\x80\x94one of the only Dark Riders to survive\xe2\x80\x94teamed up with Chimera to get revenge on Wolverine.<ref>''Wolverine'' vol 2 #97</ref> Chimera encountered Wolverine once again when he and [[Venom (Marvel Comics character)|Venom]] were lured into a trap set by her and [[Dirtnap (comics)|Dirtnap]]. She and Dirtnap were both seemingly killed in an [[Implosion (mechanical process)|implosion]].\n\nThe villains survived and attacked the [[Generation X (comics)|Generation X]] school in an attempt to kidnap the M twins, but to defend themselves the twins merged back into [[M (comics)|M]] and then merged with [[Emplate]], creating the being known as M-Plate. Chimera then captured [[Synch (comics)|Synch]] and fled along with M-Plate. Synch was later rescued by Generation X.<ref>''Generation X'' #33</ref>\n\nChimera is next seen in Madripoor killing drug runners. She is approached by the [[Madelyne Pryor|Red Queen]] to join her Sisterhood of Mutants.<ref>''Uncanny X-Men'' #503</ref>\n\nLater in Japan they dig up [[Revanche (Marvel Comics)|Kwannon]]'s body and confront Domino who is there on other business. [[Domino (comics)|Domino]] critically injures her but she gets away with the rest of the Sisterhood and Kwannon's body. After the Red Queen heals her, the Sisterhood perform a spell involving Kwannon's body and a captive [[Psylocke]], returning her to her original body.<ref>''Uncanny X-Men'' #508</ref> The Sisterhood then attack the [[X-Men]]. Chimera, along with Martinique, attacks [[Northstar (Marvel Comics)|Northstar]], [[Cyclops (Marvel Comics)|Cyclops]] and [[Dazzler (comics)|Dazzler]].<ref>''Uncanny X-Men'' #509</ref> After taking the adult X-Men out, she tries to attack [[Armor (comics)|Armor]] and X-23 only for the girls to be teleported out by Pixie. Pixie goes and fetches the Stepford Cuckoos and Elixir and Chimera attacks the Cuckoo's who respond by savagely beating her.<ref>''Uncanny X-Men'' #510</ref>\n\nSpiral then teleports the Sisterhood back to their base where they split up with Chimera going with the Red Queen to Jean Grey's burial site. There she attacks Domino only to be beaten by Colossus. When the Red Queen is defeated, Spiral teleports the Sisterhood away.<ref>''Uncanny X-Men'' #511</ref>\n\nChimera later appears as a member of a group of [[Marauders (comics)|Marauders]] that are brainwashed to attack the X-Men.<ref>''Astonishing X-Men'' #48-49</ref>\n\nAs part of the ''[[All-New, All-Different Marvel]]'' event, Chimera appears as a member of the Marauders. She assists [[Aries (comics)|Aries]], Azimuth, and Coda into pursuing Nightcrawler through the sewers in order to capture him and make him Mister Sinister's specimen.<ref>''Extraordinary X-Men'' #1</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nChimera can generate [[dragon]]-shaped flares of greenish-transparent [[ectoplasm (paranormal)|ectoplasm]]ic energy to strike her enemies on various planes of being (i.e. physical, astral, etc.). She has used the energy to move objects like telekinesis and also has some low-level telepathic abilities that allow her to sense astral projections and if she is being watched from behind. She is experienced in interdimensional travel and the use of weapons and technology from other dimensions.\n\n==Other characters==\nThere are several other characters named Chimera in the Marvel Universe.\n\n===Femizons version===\nChimera is an unnamed woman who is a member of the [[Femizons]]. She is a metamorph who can grow wings, claws, and other parts.<ref>''Captain America'' #387. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\n===Mythical chimera===\nThe [[Chimera (mythology)|chimera]] of [[Greek mythology]] appeared in Marvel Comics. It is depicted as a fire-breathing monster with the heads of a [[lion]] and a [[goat]] next to each other, the front legs of a lion, the wings of a [[dragon]], the hindquarters of a goat, and a snake-headed tail, while also possessing the power of speech. Its history of being the offspring of [[Echidna (mythology)|Echidna]] and being slain by [[Bellerophon]] is still mentioned. At some point, the Chimera was restored to life by [[Hera (Marvel Comics)|Hera]] to guard the caverns underneath New Olympus. It was accompanied in this job by a [[Cyclops]] and some [[Skeleton (undead)|Skeleton]] Warriors.<ref>''Incredible Hercules'' #138. Marvel Comics.</ref> The Chimera encountered the [[Agents of Atlas]]. When it didn't see that they were [[Olympians (Marvel Comics)|Olympians]], Chimera breathed fire at them, sparking a fight between the heroes and the minions of Hera.<ref>''Incredible Hercules'' #139.
