A professional football player whose career was cut short, he developed a costume that enabled him to fly. He started his criminal career as a mercenary assassin when he was hired to kill the original [[Spider-Woman (Jessica Drew)|Spider-Woman]].<ref>\'\'Spider-Woman\'\' #40</ref>\n\nFlying Tiger was among the superhuman individuals that were abducted by Locksmith and Tick-Tock where they were placed in Locksmith\'s prison. When in his cell, Flying Tiger felt claustrophobic. He was later freed by Spider-Woman and [[Skein (comics)|Gypsy Moth]] where Flying Tiger escaped.<ref>\'\'Spider-Woman\'\' #50</ref>\n\nGeneral Nguyen Ngoc Coy later recommended Flying Tiger to his South American rebel allies. He was later hired to abduct businessmen Regis Fussky and deposit him at a base in South America. As part of an agreement with [[Kingpin (comics)|Kingpin]], Flying Tiger is not to abduct Regis Fussky until he was out of the United States. Regis Fussky hired [[Iron Man]] ([[War Machine|James Rhodes]]) into being his bodyguard while Flying Tiger replaced the co-pilot of Regis Fussky\'s airplane. Once the airplane is over South America, Flying Tiger emerged from the cockpit and grabbed Regis Fussky while grabbing the suitcase that contained the Iron Man armor. James Rhodes escaped in a parachute as Flying Tiger delivered Regis Fussky to his employers. Flying Tiger then flirted with a woman who couldn\'t open the suitcase and threw it into the jungle. James Rhodes was able to recover the suitcase and transformed into Iron Man. During Flying Tiger\'s fight with Iron Man, the US government began dropping bombs on Colonel Perez\'s camp. Flying Tiger was caught in one of the explosions. Iron Man rescued Regis Fussky and took down the fleeing Flying Tiger.<ref>\'\'Iron Man\'\' #177</ref>\n\nDuring the \'\'[[Acts of Vengeance]]\'\', [[Doctor Doom]] used an Aggression Enhancer on Flying Tiger and other villains to attack the [[Fantastic Four]] when they stood before Congress. They were defeated by the Fantastic Four.<ref>\'\'Fantastic Four\'\' #335</ref>\n\nFlying Tiger was next seen being admitted into the [[Vault (comics)|Vault]] following a massive prison escape attempt.<ref>\'\'Avengers Spotlight\'\' #29</ref>\n\nLater, Flying Tiger joined the [[Crimson Cowl (Justine Hammer)|Crimson Cowl\'s]] [[Masters of Evil]]. The group\'s plot to blackmail the governments of the world using a weather-controlling machine was stopped by the [[Thunderbolts (comics)|Thunderbolts]].<ref>\'\'Thunderbolts\'\' #18-25</ref>\n\nLater in San Francisco, Flying Tiger was defeated by [[Armory (comics)|Armory]].<ref>\'\'Avengers: The Initiative Annual\'\' #1/2</ref>\n\nDuring the \'\'[[Fear Itself (comics)|Fear Itself]]\'\' storyline, Flying Tiger was seen trying to escape from the demolished Raft until he ran into the [[Avengers Academy]] staff.<ref>\'\'Avengers Academy\'\' #15</ref>\n\nFlying Tiger and [[Puff Adder (comics)|Puff Adder]] were watching TV when a bandaged [[Spider-Man|Peter Parker]] rolled in on a wheelchair and joined them. Parker realized that he was among the supervillains when [[Shocker (comics)|Shocker]] appeared with an updated mask that [[Tinkerer]] created for him.<ref>\'\'The Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #700.3</ref> The Black Lodge\'s Surgeon General asked Flying Tiger to help take down Spider-Man. Flying Tiger joined Puff Adder and [[Eel (comics)|Eel]] II in fighting the still-bandaged Spider-Man. During the fight, Flying Tiger tossed Spider-Man against a wall only to be later knocked out by Spider-Man. Upon [[Firebrand (Marvel Comics)|Firebrand]] setting the building on fire, Spider-Man got Flying Tiger and Eel\'s bodies out of the building.<ref>\'\'The Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #700.4</ref>\n\nDuring the \'\'[[Avengers: Standoff!]]\'\' storyline, Flying Tiger was an inmate of Pleasant Hill, a gated community established by S.H.I.E.L.D.<ref>\'\'All-New, All-Different Avengers\'\' #8</ref>\n\nFlying Tiger later appears as a member of [[Baron Zemo|Baron Helmut Zemo]]\'s "New Masters" alongside fellow villains [[Firebrand (Marvel Comics)|Firebrand]] and [[Plantman]] II. They later encounter Steve Rogers, the original Captain America, [[Free Spirit (comics)|Free Spirit]] and [[Jack Flag]]. During the fight, Flying Tiger is defeated by Jack Flag.<ref>\'\'Captain America: Steve Rogers\'\' #1</ref>\n\n==Equipment==\nFlying Tiger wears body armor under a tiger costume and is capable of powered flight.
