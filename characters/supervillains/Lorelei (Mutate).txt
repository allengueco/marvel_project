The character\'s first appearance was in \'\'X-Men #63\'\'.\n\n==Publication history==\nLorelei first appeared in \'\'[[X-Men]]\'\' vol. 1 #63 (December 1969), and was created by [[Roy Thomas]] and [[Neal Adams]].\n\nThe character subsequently appears in \'\'Avengers\'\' vol. 1 #105 (November 1972), \'\'Defenders\'\' vol. 1 #15-16 (September\xe2\x80\x93October 1974), \'\'Uncanny X-Men\'\' #104 (April 1977), \'\'Champions\'\' #17 (January 1978), \'\'Super-Villain Team-Up\'\' #14 (October 1977), \'\'Marvel Fanfare\'\' vol. 1 #1-4 (March\xe2\x80\x93September 1982), \'\'Captain America\'\' #415-417 (May\xe2\x80\x93July 1993), \'\'X-Men: The Hidden Years\'\' #4 (March 2000), \'\'Cable & Deadpool\'\' #49 (March 2008), and \'\'Marvel Comics Presents\'\' #6-7 (April\xe2\x80\x93May 2008).\n\nLorelei appeared as part of the "Savage Land Mutates" entry in the \'\'[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Deluxe Edition]]\'\' #11.\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nLorelei is a [[Savage Land]] native until [[Magneto (comics)|Magneto]] turns her into a mutated siren, whose voice can put men into a [[hypnosis|hypnotic trance]], and she is placed in his [[Savage Land Mutates]] group. However, the machine that Magneto had used to empower his Savage Land creations is eventually destroyed. As a result, Lorelei lost her siren powers.<ref>\'\'X-Men\'\' #63</ref>\n\nSome time later, she was seen with her powers somehow restored.<ref>\'\'[[Defenders (comics)|Defenders]]\'\' #15\xe2\x80\x9316</ref> She made a further appearance with the [[Brotherhood of Mutants|Brotherhood of Evil Mutants]].<ref>\'\'[[Champions (1975 team)|Champions]]\'\' #17</ref>\n\nYears later, Lorelei was seen allied with the High Technician in the Savage Land.  She battled [[Captain America]] and attempted to hypnotize the [[Falcon (comics)|Falcon]], but was quickly defeated by [[Diamondback (Rachel Leighton)|Diamondback]].<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #415 (May 1993)</ref>\n\nMuch later, she manipulated [[Ka-Zar (comics)|Ka-Zar]] to battle [[Deadpool]]. This was part of a mission to expand the dominion of the Savage Land. She uses many of the Land\'s inhabitants. Deadpool shoots her prehistoric ride and leaves her falling towards a group of dinosaurs. She survives this incident.<ref>\'\'Cable & Deadpool\'\' #49 (March 2008)</ref>\n\nLorelei helps chase out invading mercenaries.<ref>\'\'Marvel Comics Presents\'\' #6-7 (April\xe2\x80\x93May 2008)</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nLorelei can hypnotically paralyze men by singing, or manipulate them and place them under her control.\n\n==Other versions==\n[[File:Lani Ubana.jpg|thumb|upright|Lorelei controlling [[Thor (comics)|Thor]], telling him to kill [[Valkyrie (Marvel Comics)|Valkyrie]] in \'\'Ultimates 3\'\'. <br>Art by [[Joe Madureira]]]]\n\n===Ultimate Marvel===\nLorelei has appeared in the [[Ultimate Marvel|Ultimate universe]] as a member of the [[Brotherhood of Evil Mutants]].
