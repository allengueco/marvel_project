Her first appearance was in ''Generation X #53''.\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nSpoilsport was born an advanced generation mutant and is the younger sister to the mutant [[Dragonwing]]. She became a member of the Rising Sons with her brother, [[Nightwind]], Jet-Black, the Sign and Tough Love. The Rising Sun were hired to protect a man named Noy, who had in his possession a sword that was used to kill [[Adrienne Frost]]'s husband.{{issue|date=December 2012}}\n\nAdrienne wanted the sword for herself and sent the [[mercenary]] Paladin to get it, but the Rising Sons fought him off and left him for dead. [[Paladin (comics)|Paladin]] returned alongside [[Generation X (comics)|Generation X]] members [[Jubilee (comics)|Jubilee]], [[Skin (Marvel Comics)|Skin]], [[Chamber (comics)|Chamber]], [[Synch (comics)|Synch]] and [[Husk (comics)|Husk]].{{issue|date=December 2012}}\n\nEven though Spoilsport seemed rather flighty and superficial, she was an avid warrior. She attacked and bested Paladin, but was later defeated by Jubilee and Skin.{{issue|date=December 2012}}\n\nWhen the [[mentally ill]] [[Scarlet Witch]] depowered over 90% of the world's mutant population on [[Decimation (comics)|M-Day]], Spoilsport was among the many who lost her powers.<ref>confirmed in ''New Avengers #18''</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nSpoilsport is a mutant with the ability to defy gravity when skating on her inline skates. She can zoom at high speeds up walls and on ceilings without any problem, and frequently uses surprise and her agility to take her opponents down quickly.\n\n==Bibliography==\n*''Generation X'' #53-54\n*''New Avengers'' #18 (shown on screen)\n\n==References==\n{{reflist}}\n\n==External links==\n*[http://www.uncannyxmen.net/db/characters/showquestion.asp?fldAuto=1002 Uncannyxmen.net character bio on Spoilsport]\n\n[[Category:Fictional criminals]]\n[[Category:Marvel Comics mutants]]\n[[Category:Marvel Comics supervillains]]\n\n{{Marvel-villain-stub}}"
