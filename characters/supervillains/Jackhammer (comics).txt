b"{{Infobox comics character| <!--Wikipedia:WikiProject Comics--> \nimage=\n|caption=\n|character_name=Jackhammer\n|real_name=Matthew Banham\n|species=\n|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]\n|debut=''[[Daredevil (Marvel Comics series)|Daredevil]]'' #123 (July 1975)\n|creators=[[Tony Isabella]]<br>[[Bob Brown (comics)|Bob Brown]]\n|alliances=[[Hydra (comics)|HYDRA]]<br/>[[Power Tools (comics)|Power Tools]]<br/>[[Masters of Evil]]\n|aliases=\n|powers=super-strength.\n}}\n'''Jackhammer''' ('''Matthew Banham'''){{Citation needed|date=March 2008}} is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].\n\n==Publication history==\nJackhammer was first mentioned as a [[Hydra (comics)|HYDRA]] leader in ''Daredevil'' #121; he first appeared in ''Daredevil'' #123 (July 1975) and was created by [[Tony Isabella]] and [[Bob Brown (comics)|Bob Brown]].\n\nThe character subsequently appears in ''Captain America'' #371 (June 1990), #373-378 (July\xe2\x80\x93October 1990), ''Guardians of the Galaxy'' #28-29 (September\xe2\x80\x93October 1992), ''Captain America'' #412-414 (February\xe2\x80\x93April 1993), ''Thunderbolts'' #24-25 (March\xe2\x80\x93April 1999), and ''Union Jack'' #1-2 (November\xe2\x80\x93December 2006).\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nJackhammer is a costumed agent/division leader of [[Hydra (comics)|HYDRA]] when it was under the leadership of the crime lord [[Silvermane]]. When they arranged a kidnapping of [[Foggy Nelson]], [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]], [[Black Widow (Natasha Romanova)|Black Widow]], and [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] pursued them. Jackhammer was among those who fought Daredevil and was defeated.<ref>''Daredevil'' #123</ref>\n\nJackhammer later left HYDRA and gained superhuman strength from a treatment at [[Power Broker]], Inc. and started a relationship with female wrestler [[Poundcakes]] whose rebuff threatened the first date of [[Captain America]] and [[Diamondback (Rachel Leighton)|Diamondback]]. [[Anaconda (comics)|Anaconda]] and [[Asp (comics)|Asp]] rendered Jackhammer and Poundcake unconscious.<ref>''Captain America'' #371</ref>\n\nDr. [[Karl Malus]] of Power Broker, Inc. formed Power Tools with Jackhammer and other villains in their plot to capture [[Battlestar (comics)|Battlestar]] and other characters when they de-powered them. He was among the villains that fought Captain America.<ref>''Captain America'' #373-378</ref>\n\nJackhammer left Power Tools once when there was an occasion that [[Doctor Octopus]] recruited him to join his incarnation of the [[Masters of Evil]] during the ''[[The Infinity War|Infinity War]]''. He was among the villains that turned on Doctor Octopus causing him to take flight.<ref>''Guardians of the Galaxy'' #28-29</ref>\n\nHe would participate in an attack on the Thames Tunnel, threatening many civilians inside.
