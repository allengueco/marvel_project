The three brothers\' names are \'\'\'Harlan\'\'\', \'\'\'Sven\'\'\' and \'\'\'Eric\'\'\'\n\n==Publication history==\nThe Kleinstocks first appeared in \'\'[[Uncanny X-Men]]\'\' #298 (March 1993) and were created by [[Scott Lobdell]] and [[Brandon Peterson]].\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nIn their first mission with the [[Acolytes (comics)|Acolytes]], they were sent along with [[Cargill (comics)|Cargill]] and [[Unuscione|Carmella Unuscione]] to kidnap a young mutant. While attempting the kidnapping, they murdered [[Sharon Friedlander]], a former staff member at [[Professor X|Charles Xavier]]\'s [[X-Mansion|School for Gifted Youngsters]]. In the moment before she died, Sharon managed to send a [[psionic]] cry for help to Xavier. Based upon this, Xavier sent a squad of X-Men to investigate. During the battle between the Acolytes and the X-Men, Eric was shot and killed by [[Tom Corsi]], another former staff member from Xavier\'s school. After discovering the mutant they were sent to kidnap had [[Down syndrome]], the Acolytes abandoned their mission.<ref>"Uncanny X-Men" #298</ref>\n\nDespite losing their brother, Harlan and Sven continued to serve with the Acolytes. During the destruction of [[Providence (comics)|Avalon]] by [[Holocaust (Marvel Comics)|Holocaust]], the surviving two were among the Acolytes who evacuated to Earth with the X-Man [[Cyclops (Marvel Comics)|Cyclops]]. They crash landed in the [[Australian Outback]], where they managed to get to the X-Men\'s former base of operations. Once help arrived, they turned themselves over to the authorities.{{Issue|date=April 2010}}\n\nThey were able to escape prison and rejoined the Acolytes under the leadership of [[Fabian Cortez]]. These Acolytes were involved in attacks on [[Wundagore|Wundagore mountain]]. Some time later, they were involved in an attack on the [[X-Mansion]] with a group of Acolytes. At the end of the battle, they asked for sanctuary with Xavier, which was denied. They eventually rejoined the Acolytes under [[Magneto (comics)|Magneto]]\'s command, when he was given leadership over [[Genosha]].{{Issue|date=April 2010}}\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nThe Kleinstocks possess explosive plasma blasts and flight. When merged into one body, the Kleistocks possess superhuman size, strength, speed, stamina, and sturdiness.\n\n==In other media==\n===Television===\n* The Kleinstock Brothers appear in the \'\'[[Wolverine and the X-Men (TV series)|Wolverine and the X-Men]]\'\' episode "Battle Lines".
