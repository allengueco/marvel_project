The world\'s first "live streaming super-villain", she commits crimes to get more hits on her web page and is an enemy of [[Spider-Man]]. Screwball first appears in \'\'Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #559 and was created by [[Dan Slott]] and [[Marcos Mart\xc3\xadn]].\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nNot much is known about Screwball\'s past. Screwball would commit crimes while a film crew filmed her so that she can get more hits on her videos by having [[Spider-Man]] appear on it. She first encountered Spider-Man while he was tracing the "Spider-Tracer Killer."<ref>\'\'Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #559. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nScrewball later posed as Spider-Man to help Bookie where they filmed Spider-Man fighting a villain called Basher. The fight was uploaded on [[YouTube]] and watched by the patrons of the [[Bar with No Name]]. The [[Enforcers (comics)|Enforcers]] recognized what was happening and began to attack Bookie only for him to be rescued when the real Spider-Man showed up.<ref>\'\'Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #562. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nWhen Spider-Man and [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]] arrive at the Bar With No Name, Screwball is among the patrons that attack them.<ref>\'\'Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #600. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nBecause of Spider-Man\'s humiliating fight with Screwball, [[Ben Reilly]] didn\'t want to buy any of Peter Parker\'s photos.<ref>\'\'Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #608. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nDuring the "Origin of the Species" storyline, Spider-Man apprehends Screwball and [[Looter (comics)|Looter]] in Midtown in light of [[Doctor Octopus]] stealing [[Menace (Marvel Comics)|Lily Hollister]]\'s newborn child Stanley.<ref>\'\'Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #645. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\n[[Anya Corazon|Spider-Girl]] later chased Screwball and confiscated her camera to prevent her from broadcasting her latest activity before handing her over to the police.<ref>\'\'Spider-Girl\'\' Vol. 2 #1. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nScrewball later collaborated with [[Jester (Marvel Comics)|Jester]] in pranking Mayor [[J. Jonah Jameson]] which was uploaded on the Internet. Both villains were defeated by [[The Superior Spider-Man|Superior Spider-Man]] (Doctor Octopus\' mind in Spider-Man\'s body).<ref>\'\'Superior Spider-Man\'\' #6. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nScrewball later appeared in [[Las Vegas]] where she collaborated with [[Arcade (Marvel Comics)|Arcade]] who gave her the training, equipment, and exclusive streaming rights that she needed.<ref>\'\'Elektra\'\' Vol. 4 #1-3. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nScrewball is an expert gymnast.\n\n==In other media==\n* Screwball appears in \'\'[[Spider-Man (2017 TV series)|Marvel\'s Spider-Man]]\'\'. She is actually the alter-ego of [[Liz Allan]], voiced by [[Natalie Lander]]. In "Screwball Live!", Screwball sets up reckless pranks on corrupt businesses and live streams them. One of her pranks gets her and [[Randy Robertson]] nearly killed by [[Hammerhead (comics)|Hammerhead]] and [[Absorbing Man]]. However, they are both saved by Spider-Man. Realizing she was using her internet exposure recklessly, Liz takes a "hiatus" from making more videos. \n\n* Screwball appears in the 2018 \'\'[[Spider-Man (2018 video game)|Spider-Man]]\'\' video game, voiced by [[Stephanie Lemelin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.polygon.com/2018/9/7/17819416/spider-man-ps4-villains-guide|title=A guide to the Spider-Man PS4 villains|website=Polygon.com|accessdate=9 September 2018}}</ref> Screwball is part of a side mission where Spider-Man has to partake in a series of challenges before her fans murder a hostage, all while she live streams Spider-Man\'s feats. Screwball pretends to be the hostage, only for her fans to ambush Spider-Man.
