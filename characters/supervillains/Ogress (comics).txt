He hopes some of its inhabitants would survive and join him in his new kingdom of Freehold.<ref>[[David, Peter]] (w), [[McFarlane, Todd]] (a). ''The Incredible Hulk'' #345. Marvel Comics.</ref> Diane was one of only five people to survive a gamma explosion and develop superhuman powers. Diane becomes Ogress and joins the other four people into forming the [[Riot Squad (comics)|Riot Squad]], which protects Leader's Freehold base at the time when Leader had contacted a terminal illness. They first clash with Bruce Banner/Hulk shortly before the Leader provides him the location of Bruce Banner's wife, [[Betty Ross]] in exchange for Hulk killing Leader's brother, [[Madman (Marvel Comics)|Madman]].<ref>David, Peter (w). [[Purves, Jeff]] (p). ''The Incredible Hulk'' #366. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nOgress is a member of the Riot Squad when they help the [[U-Foes]] distract Hulk and the [[Pantheon (Marvel Comics)|Pantheon]] at the time when Leader was meets with [[Agamemnon (comics)|Agamemnon]].<ref>David, Peter (w). ''The Incredible Hulk'' #397. Marvel Comics.</ref> Hulk and the Pantheon's fight with the U-Foes and the Riot Squad are broken up when Agamemnon reaches an agreement with Leader.<ref>David, Peter (w). ''The Incredible Hulk #398. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nOgress was present with the Riot Squad at the time when [[Hydra (comics)|HYDRA]] invades Leader's base with the resulting conflict causing the death of Soul Man (who was in the middle of reviving [[Marlo Chandler]]).<ref>David, Peter (w). ''The Incredible Hulk'' #400. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nOgress and Hotshot fight Hulk at the time when Omnibus plans to cause destruction throughout the world and they end up defeated.<ref>''The Incredible Hulk'' #439. Marvel Comics.</ref> Ogress was among those with Hotshot when they storm Omnibus' lair and accuse him of being behind the Alliance before taking him away.<ref>David, Peter (w). ''The Incredible Hulk'' #440. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nOgress and the rest of the Riot Squad worked with the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]] to shut down the remaining bombs. The Riot Squad then judges Omnibus to be guilty of being behind the Alliance and end up sentencing Omnibus to death.<ref>''Avengers'' #397. Marvel Comics.</ref> Ogress and the rest of the Riot Squad left Omnibus in the Arctic to die.<ref>David, Peter (w). ''The Incredible Hulk'' #442. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nOgress possesses superhuman strength and durability rivaling that of the Hulk.{{fact|date=May 2018}}\n\n==In other media==\nOgress appears in ''[[The Incredible Hulk (1996 TV series)|The Incredible Hulk]]'' TV series voiced by [[Kathy Ireland]].
