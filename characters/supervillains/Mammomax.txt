He exists in Marvel\'s main [[shared universe]], known as the [[Marvel Universe]].\n\n==Publication history==\nMammomax\'s first appearance was \'\'[[X-Men]]\'\' vol. 2 #161 (November, 2004), and he was created by writer [[Chuck Austen]] and artist [[Salvador Larroca]]. He appeared in issues #162-164 (November 2004-January 2005).\n\nThe character subsequently appeared in \'\'Uncanny X-Men\'\' #463-466 (October 2005-January 2006), \'\'Decimation: House of M - The Day After\'\' #1 (January 2006), \'\'X-Men: The 198 Files\'\' #1 (March 2006), \'\'X-Men: The 198\'\' #1-5 (March\xe2\x80\x93July 2006), \'\'Uncanny X-Men\'\' #469 (April 2006), \'\'X-Men\'\' #183  (April 2006), \'\'Civil War: X-Men\'\' #1-2 (September\xe2\x80\x93October 2006), \'\'New X-Men\'\' #33-34 (February\xe2\x80\x93March 2007), and \'\'X-Men: Messiah Complex - Mutant Files\'\' (December 2007).\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nWhen Mammomax first appeared, he was part of the [[Brotherhood of Evil Mutants]] alongside [[Avalanche (comics)|Avalanche]], [[Sabretooth (comics)|Sabretooth]], [[Exodus (comics)|Exodus]], [[Juggernaut (comics)|Juggernaut]], [[Nocturne (Talia Wagner)|Nocturne]], and [[Black Tom Cassidy]]. He participated in an attack on the X-Mansion, which caused massive property damage and the deaths of at least two of its residents.{{Issue|date=April 2010}}\n\n[[Iceman (comics)|Iceman]] defeated him. The entire Brotherhood ended up sucked into the black hole within [[Xorn]]\'s head,{{Issue|date=April 2010}} which apparently leads to the Mojoverse. The Brotherhood bargained with [[Mojo (comics)|Mojo]], apparently giving him Juggernaut and Nocturne in price for their freedom.{{Issue|date=April 2010}}\n\nMammomax next appears after [[Decimation (comics)|M-Day]], on the run from members of the Sapien League with [[Erg (comics)|Erg]] and [[Peepers (Marvel Comics)|Peepers]].<ref>Decimation: The Day After</ref> At the Institute\'s front gates, the X-Men save him from being burned alive.{{Issue|date=April 2010}} He took up residence on the Institute\'s grounds with other members of the 198. He also forms a friendship with Erg, [[Fever Pitch (comics)|Fever Pitch]], and [[Sack (comics)|Sack]].{{Issue|date=April 2010}} During an outing into Salem Center, he attacks a boy for calling him "[[The Elephant Man (film)|Elephant Man]]", but a "tracking chip", implanted in his head by the [[Office of National Emergency]] (O*N*E), disables him before he could do any damage.<ref>\'\'X-Men: The 198\'\' #3</ref> Mammomax has the device removed by [[Mister M (comics)|Mister M]]. The other members of the 198 also have their chips neutralized.<ref name=XMEN198-4>\'\'X-Men: The 198\'\' #4</ref> Mammomax follows Mr. M during his exodus from the [[X-Mansion|Xavier Institute]].<ref name=XMEN198-4/> He eventually rejoins the Brotherhood.<ref name=NXMEN34>\'\'New X-Men\'\' #34</ref>\n\nMammomax was later captured by the Facility, the [[Weapon Plus]] offshoot that was responsible for the creation of [[X-23]]. He is used as a test subject for a [[Predator X (comics)|monster]] created by the Facility staff. Predator X consumes and kills him.<ref>New X-Men vol. 2 #33-34</ref> A picture of his skeleton is shown to [[Mercury (Marvel Comics)|Mercury]], who had also been taken by the organization.<ref name=NXMEN34/>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nMammomax resembles a bipedal elephant with hands, because of this he is as strong as an elephant and also possess a pair of large ivory tusks which are as durable as an elephant\'s. Mammomax has invulnerable skin and can also produce a powerful stomach acid, which he can spit out of his mouth and use as a projectile attack.\n\n==Other versions==\n===House of M===\nIn the \'\'[[House of M]]\'\' reality, Mammomax is a member of the Genoshan Black Ops version of the [[Marauders (comics)|Marauders]].
