b"{{Infobox comics character <!--Wikipedia:WikiProject Comics-->\n|image=\n|caption=\n|character_name=Diamondhead\n|real_name=Arch Dyker\n|species=\n|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]\n|debut=''[[Nova (Richard Rider)|Nova]] #3'' (Nov 1976)\n|creators=[[Marv Wolfman]] and [[Sal Buscema]]\n|status=\n|alliances=Terrible Trio\n|previous_alliances=\n|aliases=\n|relatives=\n|powers=Diamond body granted super strength and invulnerability\n}}\n\n'''Diamondhead''' is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].\n\n==Publication history==\nDiamondhead first appeared in ''[[Nova (Richard Rider)|Nova]]'' vol 1 #3 (Nov 1976), and was created by [[Marv Wolfman]] and [[Sal Buscema]].\n\nThe character subsequently appears in ''Nova'' #6-8 (Feb-Apr 1977), #10 (June 1977), #22-25 (Nov 1978-May 1979), ''Fantastic Four'' #206 (May 1979), #208-209 (July-Aug 1979), ''ROM'' #24 (Nov 1981), ''Quasar'' #16 (Nov 1990), #20 (Mar 1991), ''The New Warriors Annual'' #1 (1991), ''Nova'' vol 2 #1 (Jan 1994), #10 (Oct 1994), ''Nova: The Human Rocket'' #2 (June 1999), ''Civil War: Front Line'' #9 (Feb 2007), and ''Nova'' vol 4 #2 (July 2007)\n\nDiamondhead received an entry in the ''All-New Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe A-Z'' #3 (2006), and the ''Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe A to Z'' HC vol. 02 (2008).\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nDiamondhead was mutated by a diamond-powered laser while attempting to steal some precious gems. He developed a hard, diamond-like body that was superstrong. He used his new abilities to enhance his criminal career.\n\nAfter being defeated by [[Nova (Richard Rider)|Nova]],<ref>''Nova'' vol 1 #3 (Nov 1976)</ref> he forms the criminal gang Terrible Trio with [[Condor (comics)|Condor]] and [[Powerhouse (comics)#Powerhouse (Rieg Davan)|Powerhouse]].<ref>''Nova'' vol 1 #7</ref> After Condor is defeated by the [[Sphinx (comics)|Sphinx]], Diamondhead allies with the Sphinx and [[Doctor Sun]]. Along with his allies, Nova, and the [[Champions of Xandar]], Diamondhead goes to [[Xandar]] on Nova's spacecraft.<ref>''Nova'' vol 1 #22-25</ref><ref>''Fantastic Four'' vol 1 #206</ref>\n\nOnce there, Diamondhead betrays the Nova Corps to the [[Skrull]]s. This causes the death of one of the Champions, and Diamondhead is left to float in space.<ref>''ROM'' issue 24</ref>\n\nLater, Diamondhead was seen as a prisoner on the [[Stranger (comics)|Stranger]]'s laboratory world.<ref>''Quasar'' #16</ref> He escapes and returns to Earth.{{Issue|date=October 2011}}\n\nFollowing the [[Civil War (comics)|Stamford incident]], the identities of the [[New Warriors]] were publicly leaked, including Nova's.{{Issue|date=November 2009}} Diamondhead uses this information to locate Nova's parents' house and patiently waits until his nemesis comes home. When Nova returns to Earth after the [[Annihilation (comics)|Annihilation War]], Diamondhead attacks him. Nova easily defeats Diamondhead and takes him back to prison.<ref>''Nova'' #2 (July 2007)</ref>\n\nAs part of the ''[[Marvel NOW!]]'' event, Diamondhead ambushes the new [[Nova (Sam Alexander)|Nova]], but is easily defeated and left stranded in the desert.<ref>''Marvel NOW Point One'' #1 (2012)</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nDiamondhead's body is basically one large diamond, making him incredibly strong and incredibly resistant to injury and harm.
