The character did not appear again for some time, until \'\'The Incredible Hulk\'\' #341 (March 1988), and he then appeared in \'\'Marvel Year-in-Review \'92\'\', \'\'The Amazing Spider-Man: Chaos in Calgary\'\' #4 (February 1993), \'\'Captain America\'\' #413 (March 1993), and \'\'New Warriors\'\' #36 (June 1993). He disappeared again for a time, before appearing in \'\'She-Hulk\'\' #10 (February 2005), \'\'Gravity\'\' #1 (August 2005), \'\'Wolverine\'\' #30 (September 2005), \'\'Spider-Man Unlimited\'\' #12 (January 2006), \'\'Underworld\'\' #3 (June 2006), and \'\'Punisher War Journal\'\' #13-15 (January\xe2\x80\x93March 2008).\n\nMan-Bull received an entry in the original \'\'[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe]]\'\' #6, and in the \'\'All-New Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe A to Z: Update\'\' #1 (2007).\n\n==Fictional character biography==\n\'\'\'William "Bill" Taurens\'\'\' was born in [[Camden, New Jersey]]. He was hired by Mister Kline to round up people to test an experimental serum (taken from [[Cattle|bull]]s) made by his agent The Professor. Although assisted by Itch and Freak Face, his attempts were thwarted by [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]]. As a result, Bill ended up being a guinea pig for the serum which turned him into a [[humanoid]] bull.<ref>\'\'Daredevil\'\' #78</ref>\n\nWith his new powers, Man-Bull attempted to take his revenge on Daredevil and the two have clashed on several different occasions.<ref>\'\'Daredevil\'\' #79, 95-96</ref> He also clashed with [[Tigra|The Cat]] in a bar fight.<ref>\'\'Claws of the Cat\'\' #4</ref> Man-Bull was later recruited by [[Melter]] and [[Whiplash (comics)|Whiplash]] to join Black Lama\'s [[Death Squad (comics)|Death Squad]] where they ran afoul of [[Iron Man]] at a convention.<ref>\'\'Iron Man\'\' #72</ref>\n\nMan-Bull eventually began to turn savage; he lost the power of speech, sprouted a tail, and grew more inhuman. In this state, he was encountered by the [[Hulk (comics)|Grey Hulk]].<ref>\'\'Incredible Hulk\'\' #341</ref> As Man-Bull continued to deteriorate, he was recruited by the [[Wizard (Marvel Comics)|Wizard]] to join his [[Frightful Four]] alongside himself, [[Trapster]], and [[Dreadknight]]. They attacked a rodeo arena and ran afoul of [[Spider-Man]] and Turbine.<ref>\'\'Spider-Man: Chaos in Calgary\'\' #4</ref>\n\nMan-Bull later regained the power of speech somehow and joined [[Armadillo (comics)|Armadillo]], [[Equinox (comics)|Equinox]], [[Hypno-Hustler]], [[Schizoid Man (comics)|Chip Martin]], and [[Big Wheel (comics)|Jackson Wheele]] at a Villains Anonymous meeting.<ref>\'\'Spider-Man Unlimited\'\' #12</ref>\n\nMan-Bull later joined the [[Constrictor (comics)|Constrictor]], [[Tombstone (comics)|Tombstone]], [[Warhawk (Marvel Comics)|Warhawk]], and a number of [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] agents, in raiding an [[Advanced Idea Mechanics|AIM]] facility which was working on a Null android from technology stolen from [[Mister Fantastic|Reed Richards]].<ref>\'\'Wolverine\'\' Vol. 3 #30</ref>\n\nLater returning to villainy and now sprouting green-hair, he returned to New York City and fought the [[Thing (comics)|Thing]].<ref>\'\'Gravity\'\' #1</ref>\n\nWhen [[Kraven the Hunter (Alyosha Kravinoff)|Al Kraven]] (the son of [[Kraven the Hunter]]) began collecting a [[zoo]] of animal-themed superhumans, Man-Bull is clearly seen in one of the cages.<ref>\'\'[[Punisher War Journal]]\'\' Vol. 2 #13</ref> He later battled [[Grizzly (comics)|Grizzly]] who knocked him out and snapped off his left horn (yet the horn somehow regenerated by the next appearances).<ref>\'\'Punisher War Journal\'\' Vol. 2 #13-15</ref>\n\nDuring the \'\'[[Dark Reign (comics)|Dark Reign]]\'\' storyline, Man-Bull was among the villains analyzed by [[Quasimodo (comics)|Quasimodo]] for [[Green Goblin|Norman Osborn]].<ref>\'\'Dark Reign Files\'\'</ref> When imprisoned at The Raft, Man-Bull later fought [[Absorbing Man]] in the Annual Raft Boxing Tournament and lost to him in the final round.<ref>\'\'Dark Reign: Lethal Legion\'\' #2</ref>\n\nDuring the \'\'[[Fear Itself (comics)|Fear Itself]]\'\' storyline, Man-Bull is among the villains that escapes from the Raft after what [[Juggernaut (comics)|Juggernaut]], in the form of Kuurth: Breaker of Stone, did to it.<ref>\'\'Thunderbolts\'\' #158</ref> When he, [[Basilisk (comics)|Basilisk]], and [[Griffin (Marvel Comics)|Griffin]] are seen robbing a bank, [[Hercules (Marvel Comics)|Hercules]] arrives and discovers that the fourth person with the villains is actually [[Hecate (Marvel Comics)|Hecate]]. When a resurrected Kyknos ends up fighting Hercules, Man-Bull and Basilisk flee. After Hercules recovers from his fight with Kyknos, he and Griffin managed to seek out Basilisk and Man-Bull and convince them to help fight Kyknos and Hecate. The villains approach Hecate and Kyknos using a ruse involving Hercules being turned to stone. Hercules quickly revives and saves the villains by killing Kyknos. Hecate later escapes.<ref>\'\'Herc\'\' #3-6</ref>\n\nFollowing the \'\'[[Avengers vs. X-Men]]\'\' storyline, Man-Bull was among the villains taking part in the riot at a prison. [[Rogue (comics)|Rogue]] and [[Mimic (comics)|Mimic]] were the only ones to stop the riot where they copied the powers of Armadillo, Equinox, and Man-Bull to do so.<ref>\'\'X-Men: Legacy\'\' #275</ref>\n\nMan-Bull later robs an armored truck and is possibly fatally shot by the [[Punisher]].<ref> {{Cite comic|writer = Chip Zdarsky|penciller = Jason Latour|inker = Jason Latour|colorist = Jason Latour|letterer = Travis Lanham|editor = Wil Moss|story = Double\'s Jeopardy|title = Howard the Duck|volume = 5|issue = #3|date = 13 May 2015|publisher = Marvel Comics|location = United States}} </ref>\n\nAs part of the \'\'[[All-New, All-Different Marvel]]\'\', Man-Bull encountered the Emerald Warlock on [[Santorini]] and infused him with the energies that caused him to believe that he was the actual [[Minotaur]]. With some help from Hecate, [[Scarlet Witch]] agreed to help Man-Bull.<ref>\'\'Scarlet Witch\'\' Vol. 2 #2</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nMan-Bull has superhuman strength, agility and endurance. His dense flesh gives him a high degree of resistance to physical and energy attacks.
