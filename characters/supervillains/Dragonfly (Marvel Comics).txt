b'{{Notability|date=August 2018}}\n{{In-universe|date=August 2018}}\n{{Infobox comics character <!--Wikipedia:WikiProject Comics-->\n|image=\n|caption=\n|character_name=Dragonfly\n|real_name=Veronica Dultry\n|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]\n|debut=\'\'X-Men\'\' #94 (1975)\n|creators=[[Len Wein]]<br>[[Chris Claremont]]\n|alliances=[[Ani-Men]]<br>[[Femizons]]<br>[[Masters of Evil]]\n|aliases=\n|powers=Flight<br>Hypnotic gaze\n}}\n\'\'\'Dragonfly\'\'\' (\'\'\'Veronica Dultry\'\'\') is a fictional [[supervillain]]ess appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].\n\n==Publication history==\nDragonfly first appeared in \'\'[[X-Men]]\'\' #94-95 (August\xe2\x80\x93October 1975), and was created by [[Chris Claremont]] and [[Len Wein]].\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nVeronica Dultry is endowed with superhuman powers by [[Maggia (comics)|Maggia]] [[scientist]]s in order to serve the [[Boss (crime)|crime lord]] [[Count Nefaria]] among the second group of [[Ani-Men]]. The Ani-Men take control of the [[NORAD]] base at Mount Valhalla, but are defeated by the [[X-Men]],<ref>\'\'X-Men\'\' #94-95</ref> and imprisoned in the mutant research complex at [[Muir Island]]. She escapes shortly after when [[Erik the Red (comics)|Erik the Red]] breaks into the complex.<ref>\'\'X-Men\'\' #104</ref>\n\nShe is later abducted by the alien [[Stranger (comics)|Stranger]] to his laboratory world, where she and other abductees are manipulated by the [[Overmind (comics)|Overmind]] into battling [[Quasar (comics)|Quasar]].<ref>\'\'Quasar\'\' #14-16</ref> After she returns to Earth, she mutates further due to the Stranger\'s experiments, but [[Ant-Man (Scott Lang)|Ant-Man]] aids her in returning to her normal appearance.<ref>\'\'Iron Man Annual\'\' #12</ref>\n\nLater, she joins [[Superia]]\'s [[Femizons]] and battles [[Captain America]] and [[Paladin (comics)|Paladin]].<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #389-392</ref> Dragonfly and several other former Femizons battle Captain America again during an [[Advanced Idea Mechanics|A.I.M.]] weapons demonstration.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #411-413</ref> \n\nMuch later, Dragonfly is invited to join the [[Crimson Cowl]]\'s [[Masters of Evil]] where she battles the [[Thunderbolts (comics)|Thunderbolts]]. They are defeated and sent to prison.<ref>\'\'Thunderbolts\'\' #24-25</ref> \n\nIn new battle armor, she is sent to attack [[Underworld (comics)|Jackie Dio]] alongside several other criminals. Dio quickly defeats the villains by blowing up the building they were in.<ref>\'\'Underworld\'\' #4-5</ref>\n\nDuring the "Opening Salvo" part of the \'\'[[Secret Empire (comics)|Secret Empire]]\'\' storyline, Dragonfly is recruited by [[Helmut Zemo|Baron Helmut Zemo]] to join the Army of Evil.<ref>\'\'Captain America: Steve Rogers\'\' #16</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nDragonfly can fly and possesses a hypnotic gaze.
