b'{{Infobox comics character\n|image=[[Image:Black racer marvel.jpg|191x289px]] -->\n|caption=Black Racer, art by [[Kieron Dwyer]].\n|character_name=Black Racer\n|real_name=Ariana\n|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]\n|debut=\'\'[[Captain America (comic book)|Captain America]]\'\' #337 (January 1988)\n|creators=[[Mark Gruenwald]]<br>[[Tom Morgan (comics)|Tom Morgan]]\n|alliances=[[Serpent Society]]<br>[[Serpent Squad]]\n|aliases=\n|powers=Ability to move at superhuman speeds\n|}}\n\'\'\'Black Racer\'\'\' (\'\'\'Ariana\'\'\') is a [[fictional character]], a [[supervillain]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. She first appeared in \'\'[[Captain America (comic book)|Captain America]]\'\' #337, dated January 1988, and was created by writer [[Mark Gruenwald]] and artist [[Tom Morgan (comics)|Tom Morgan]]. The character is usually depicted as a member of the second [[Serpent Society]]. \n\nBlack Racer, along with [[Copperhead (comics)|Copperhead]], [[List of Marvel Comics characters: F#Fer-de-Lance|Fer-de-Lance]] and  [[Puff Adder (comics)|Puff Adder]], showed up using the name [[Serpent Society]],  using the guise to infiltrate the real Serpent Society on behalf of [[Viper (Madame Hydra)|Viper]]. Black Racer aided Viper\'s plans in turning the president into a reptile, allowing her to take control of the United States. The plans were foiled by [[Captain America]] and his allies, including various Serpent Society members still loyal to the original leader [[Sidewinder (comics)|Sidewinder]]. She remained a member of the group when the Serpent Society was reorganized into "Serpent Society".\n\n==Publication history==\nBlack Racer first appeared in \'\'[[Captain America (comic book)|Captain America]]\'\' #337-338 (January\xe2\x80\x93February 1988), and was created by [[Mark Gruenwald]] and [[Tom Morgan (comics)|Tom Morgan]].\n\nThe character subsequently appears in \'\'Captain America\'\' #341-342 (May\xe2\x80\x93June 1988), \'\'Uncanny X-Men Annual\'\' #13 (1989), \'\'Captain America\'\' #365 (December 1989), #367 (February 1990), #379 (November 1990), \'\'Quasar\'\' #17 (December 1990), \'\'Captain America\'\' #380-382 (December 1990-February 1991), and \'\'Captain America\'\' v.2 #30-31 (October\xe2\x80\x93November 2004).\n\nBlack Racer appeared as part of the "Serpent Society" entry in the \'\'[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Update \'89]]\'\' #6.\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nA quiet woman with super speed, Black Racer first joined the fourth [[Serpent Squad]], robbing a casino in [[Las Vegas, Nevada|Las Vegas]].<ref name="Captain America #337">\'\'Captain America\'\' #337</ref>  [[Sidewinder (comics)|Sidewinder]] took note of this, and invited the Serpent Squad to join his [[Serpent Society]].<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #341</ref>  They accepted, but were really just involved in a ploy to get [[Viper (Madame Hydra)|Viper]] into the leadership position of the Society. Black Racer was among the several members to side with Viper and betray Sidewinder.<ref>\'\'Captain America Annual\'\' #10</ref> She was ordered by Viper to search all the hospitals in the area for the injured former leader.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #342</ref>  However, after Viper abandoned the group, Black Racer continued to serve with the Society under leadership of [[Cobra (Marvel Comics)|Cobra]].<ref>\'\'Uncanny X-men Annual\'\' #13</ref><ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #365-367</ref> She also participated in a race between [[super hero]]es and villains with super speed.<ref>\'\'Quasar\'\' #78</ref>  However, she disappeared for quite some time, only resurfacing recently alongside the rest of the Serpent Society as they sought to exact their revenge on [[Diamondback (Rachel Leighton)|Diamondback]].<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #379-382</ref>\n\nShe has been seen as member of the [[Serpent Society]] under Cobra\'s leadership. After the group had captured and chained [[Captain America]] and [[Diamondback (Rachel Leighton)|Diamondback]] (really an L.M.D.) in this underground New York headquarters the pair escaped. [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] subsequently took Black Racer and the rest of the Society into custody.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' vol. 4 #30-31</ref>\n\nShe has been sometimes depicted as [[Caucasian race|Caucasian]],<ref name="Captain America #337" /> and other times as [[African-American]].<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #379</ref>\n\nAs part of the \'\'[[All-New, All-Different Marvel]]\'\' event, Black Racer appears as a member of [[Viper (Marvel Comics)|Viper]]\'s Serpent Society under its new name of Serpent Solutions. She assisted Cottonmouth and Copperhead into attacking Diamondback. When [[Falcon (comics)|Captain America]] fought them to help Diamondback, he learned too late that Diamondback has joined up with Serpent Solutions where she knocks him out and brings him to Serpent Solution\'s base.<ref>\'\'Captain America: Sam Wilson\'\' #4</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nBlack Racer is capable of moving at super speeds and is able to make multiple attacks.\n\n==Other versions==\n===Ultimate Marvel===\nIn the [[Ultimate Marvel|Ultimate universe]], Black Racer is a member of the gang called the Serpent Skulls. She serves as a lieutenant, and similar to her main counterpart, has super speed.
