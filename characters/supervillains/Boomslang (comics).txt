b'{{Infobox comics character\n|character_name=Boomslang\n|image=\n|caption=\n|real_name=\n|publisher=[[Marvel Comics]]\n|debut=\'\'[[Captain America (comic book)|Captain America]]\'\' #341 (May 1988)\n|creators=[[Mark Gruenwald]]<br />[[Kieron Dwyer]]\n|alliances=[[Serpent Society]]\n|aliases=\n|powers=Expert thrower<br />Wields "serpent-rangs"\n|}}\n\'\'\'Boomslang\'\'\' is a fictional [[Australian]] [[supervillain]] appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]]. The character is depicted most notably as a member of the [[Serpent Society]].\n\n==Publication history==\nBoomslang was created by [[Mark Gruenwald]] and [[Kieron Dwyer]], and first appeared in \'\'[[Captain America (comic book)|Captain America]]\'\' #341 in May 1988.\n\nThe character subsequently appears in \'\'Captain America\'\' #342-344 (June\xe2\x80\x93August 1988), \'\'Uncanny X-Men Annual\'\' #13 (1989), \'\'Captain America\'\' #367 (February 1990), #372 (July 1990), and #412-413 (February\xe2\x80\x93March 1993).\n\nBoomslang appeared as part of the "Serpent Society" entry in the \'\'[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Update \'89]]\'\' #6.\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nBoomslang infiltrated the [[Serpent Society]] along with [[Coachwhip (comics)|Coachwhip]] and [[Rock Python]] after [[Viper (Madame Hydra)|Viper]]\'s invasion, and stayed with the group for a short time. He is an excellent hand-to-hand combat fighter, and uses snake-shaped sickles called "serpent-rangs" as his weapon of choice. He was notoriously incompetent among the other members of the Serpent Society, and many [[super hero]]es couldn\'t take him seriously.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #341-342</ref>\n\nHe, along with [[Cobra (Marvel Comics)|Cobra]] and [[Copperhead (Marvel Comics)|Copperhead]], were sent by Viper to poison the water supply, turning civilians into snake creatures. They were eventually found out by [[Diamondback (Rachel Leighton)|Diamondback]] and [[Captain America]], and Boomslang was quickly taken care of by Captain America\'s shield.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #343-344</ref> During the battle against the [[X-Men]], he went up against [[Wolverine (character)|Wolverine]], who defeated him quite easily.<ref>\'\'Uncanny X-Men Annual\'\' #13</ref> He was later sent by the group to watch over Diamondback, but when Captain America discovered his presence, Boomslang ran and was eventually gunned down by a group of teenage thugs. Captain America immediately rushed Boomslang to the hospital.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #372</ref> During the [[Advanced Idea Mechanics|AIM]] Weapons Expo, Boomslang was seen among the several villains attending the fighting tournament between many super heroes and villains.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #411-413</ref>\n\nAs part of the \'\'[[All-New, All-Different Marvel]]\'\', Boomslang appears as a member of [[Viper (Marvel Comics)|Viper]]\'s Serpent Society under its new name Serpent Solutions.<ref>\'\'Captain America: Sam Wilson\'\' #4</ref>\n\nDuring the "Opening Salvo" part of the \'\'[[Secret Empire (comics)|Secret Empire]]\'\' storyline, Boomslang was with Serpent Solutions at the time when they are recruited by [[Helmut Zemo|Baron Helmut Zemo]] to join his Army of Evil.<ref>\'\'Captain America: Steve Rogers\'\' #16</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nBoomslang uses two snake-shaped "boomerangs".
