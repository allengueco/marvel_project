He is the brother of [[Diamondback (Rachel Leighton)|Diamondback]].\n\n==Publication history==\n{{expand-section|date=December 2016}}\nThe Daniel Leighton version of Cutthroat first appeared in \'\'[[Marvel Team-Up]]\'\' (1st series) #89 and was created by [[Chris Claremont]], [[Michael Netzer]], and [[Rich Buckler]].\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nDanny Leighton was born in [[Austin, Texas]]. He was a member of the Savage Crims, a New York street gang, as a young man.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #400</ref>  He later became an assassin and was (as his first high-profile job) hired by Amos Jardine to kill [[Spider-Man]], in place of the mad killer [[Arcade (Marvel Comics)|Arcade]]. Cutthroat battled [[Nightcrawler (comics)|Nightcrawler]], who had gotten wind of the plan, and Spider-Man, and they defeated him with some secret assistance from Arcade himself.<ref>\'\'Marvel Team-Up\'\' #89</ref>\n\nMany years later, he defeated [[Mangler (comics)|Mangler]] and [[Deathstrike (comics)|Deathstrike]] in a fight to the death to become the [[Red Skull]]\'s new chief operative, replacing [[Crossbones (comics)|Crossbones]].<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #395</ref>  He underwent training by the [[Taskmaster (comics)|Taskmaster]].<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #396</ref> He tried to persuade [[Blackwing#Blackwing I|Blackwing]] and [[Jack O\'Lantern (Marvel Comics)#Steven Levins|Jack O\'Lantern]] to help him free [[Mother Night (comics)|Mother Night]] from the police.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #398</ref> He initiated a brief affair with Mother Night.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #402</ref> With the [[Skeleton Crew (comics)|Skeleton Crew]], he battled Crossbones and [[Diamondback (Rachel Leighton)|Diamondback]]. He realized that Diamondback was his sister, Rachel,<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #405-406</ref> and revealed his true identity to her.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #407</ref> \n\nWhen Crossbones returned, Cutthroat feared he would be made redundant and so planned to kill Crossbones in his sleep.  However, Mother Night alerted Crossbones to the plan, and so Crossbones was waiting for the "ambush" and slit Cutthroat\'s throat and he was seemingly killed.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #408</ref> The Red Skull even inspected the apparent corpse.<ref>\'\'Captain America\'\' #409</ref>  However, unknown circumstances show that he is alive and was an inmate in the Raft where he escaped with a lot of other convicts.<ref>\'\'New Avengers\'\' vol. 1 #1</ref> He was later recaptured.{{issue|date=December 2010}}\n\nThe [[Hood (comics)|Hood]] has hired him as part of his criminal organization to take advantage of the split in the superhero community caused by the [[Superhuman Registration Act]].{{issue|date=December 2010}} He helped them fight the New Avengers but was defeated by [[Doctor Strange]].{{issue|date=December 2010}}\n\nAs part of the Hood\'s gang, he later joins the fight against the [[Skrull]] [[Secret Invasion|invading force]] in New York City.<ref>\'\'Secret Invasion\'\' #6</ref> He joins with the Hood\'s gang in an attack on the New Avengers, who were expecting the [[Dark Avengers]] instead.<ref>\'\'New Avengers\'\' #50</ref>\n\nDuring the "Search for Tony Stark" arc, Cutthroat rejoined Hood\'s gang and assisted in the attack on Castle Doom.<ref>\'\'Invincible Iron Man\'\' #597. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\n===Hobgoblin\'s Cutthroat===\nWhile regaining his franchises, [[Roderick Kingsley]] sold one of Cutthroat\'s old costumes to an unnamed criminal to become his version of Cutthroat.<ref>\'\'Spider-Woman\'\' Vol. 6 #13</ref>\n\n==Powers and abilities==\nCutthroat is a master at various forms of unarmed combat, having received special combat training from the [[Taskmaster (comics)|Taskmaster]]. He is also highly skilled in throwing bladed weapons. Cutthroat also wears lightweight body armor, and uses a sword and [[adamantium]]-edged knives. He carries a [[Gyrojet|gun that fires rockets]] that explode on contact; upon exploding, some rockets trigger a "sonic screamer" that induces pain in nearby victims.
