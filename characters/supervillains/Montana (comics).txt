Brice\'\'\') is a fictional character appearing in [[American comic book]]s published by [[Marvel Comics]].\n\n==Publication history==\nMontana first appeared in \'\'[[The Amazing Spider-Man]]\'\' #10 (March 1964), and was created by [[Stan Lee]] and [[Steve Ditko]].\n\nThe character subsequently appears in \'\'The Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #14 (July 1964), #19 (December 1964), \'\'The Amazing Spider-Man Annual\'\' #1 (1964), \'\'Marvel Team-Up\'\' #39-40 (November\xe2\x80\x93December 1975), \'\'The Spectacular Spider-Man\'\' #19-20 (June\xe2\x80\x93July 1978), \'\'Dazzler\'\' #7-8 (October\xe2\x80\x93September 1981), \'\'Marvel Team-Up\'\' #138 (February 1984), \'\'Tales of the Marvels: Inner Demons\'\' #1 (1996), \'\'Civil War: War Crimes\'\' #1 (February 2007), \'\'Daredevil\'\' #99-100 (September\xe2\x80\x93October 2007), #102 (January 2008), and \'\'The Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #562-563 (August 2008).\n\nMontana appeared as part of the "Enforcers" entry in the \'\'[[Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe|Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Deluxe Edition]]\'\' #4.\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nJackson Brice was born in [[Bozeman, Montana]]. Along with [[Fancy Dan]] (Daniel Brito) and [[Ox (comics)|Ox]] (Raymond Bloch), he was a founding member of the [[Enforcers (comics)|Enforcers]].{{Issue|date=January 2011}} He has great proficiency with the [[lasso|lariat]].\n\nMontana, Fancy Dan and Ox make their first appearance under the employ of the [[Big Man (comics)|Big Man]] ([[Frederick Foswell]]). During this time, they have their first run-in with their longtime nemesis [[Spider-Man]]. During their first fight against the web-slinger, Montana\'s lasso skills initially prove successful but Spider-Man defeats the trio.<ref>{{cite comic| writer = [[Stan Lee]]| artist = [[Steve Ditko]]| colorist = | letterer = | editor = | story = The Enforcers!| title = [[The Amazing Spider-Man]]| volume = 1| issue = 10| date = March 1964| publisher = [[Marvel Comics]]| location = | page = | panel = | id =}}</ref>\n\nOver the next couple of years, Montana and the team would be employed by [[Lightmaster]] in one of his many schemes, again bringing them into conflict with Spider-Man, with similar results.{{Issue|date=May 2011}} They would then lend their services to Tech-Master in his revenge plot against Harry S. Osgood, only to be defeated by Dazzler.{{Issue|date=May 2011}} Montana and the team would also go up against [[She-Hulk]] at one point.{{Issue|date=May 2011}}\n\nFollowing the 2006 "[[Civil War (comics)|Civil War]]" storyline, Montana, [[Ox (comics)|Ox]] (Ronald Bloch) and Fancy Dan reunite to work for [[Mister Fear]], which pits them directly against [[Daredevil (Marvel Comics character)|Daredevil]].{{Issue|date=January 2011}} After Mister Fear\'s arrest, the Enforcers are taken in by the [[Hood (comics)|Hood]]\'s organization.{{Issue|date=January 2011}}\n\nFollowing the events of the 2008 "[[Spider-Man: Brand New Day]]" storyline, the Enforcers are patrons at the [[Bar With No Name]]. They take bets with a person calling himself "The Bookie", over whether Spider-Man will show up to battle "Basher", an unknown villain who claimed to have fought Spider-Man. Spider-Man shows up, but is revealed to be [[Screwball (comics)|Screwball]] in disguise when the real web-head shows up. The Enforcers decide to get revenge on The Bookie, capturing him. The Bookie\'s father calls Spider-Man for assistance, and he agrees to help. Spider-Man defeats Fancy Dan and Montana.<ref>\'\'Amazing Spider-Man\'\' #562. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nIn the story arc "Kill To Be You", Montana is the right-hand man of the [[Kingpin (character)|Kingpin]] (Wilson Fisk), and shows contempt toward the [[Phil Urich|Hobgoblin]] (Phil Urich). When Spider-Man and the [[Black Cat (Marvel Comics)|Black Cat]] come to steal back the Reverbium, Montana falls out a window to his death while Hobgoblin saves Kingpin. Hobgoblin cruelly joked that "he\'ll be missed by everyone \xe2\x80\x93 except the pavement".<ref>{{cite comic| writer = [[Dan Slott]]| | penciller = [[Humberto Ramos]]| inker = Carlos Cuevas, Joseph Damon| colorist = | letterer = | editor = | story = Big Time (part 4) | title = [[The Amazing Spider-Man]]| volume = 1| issue = 651| date = March 2011| publisher = [[Marvel Comics]]| location = | page = | panel = | id =}}</ref>\n\nDuring the 2016 - 2017 "[[Dead No More: The Clone Conspiracy]]" storyline, Montana is among the cloned villains by [[Jackal (Marvel Comics)|Miles Warren]]\'s company New U Technologies.<ref>{{cite comic| writer = [[Dan Slott]]| | penciller = [[Jim Cheung]]| inker = John Dell| colorist = | letterer = | editor = | story = | title = [[Clone Conspiracy]]| volume = 1| issue = 2| date = January 2017| publisher = [[Marvel Comics]]| location = | page = | panel = | id =}}</ref> He is involved in a fight with the other cloned supervillains until it is broken up by the clone of the [[Prowler (comics)|Prowler]].<ref>{{cite comic| writer = [[Sean Ryan (comics)|Sean Ryan]]| | penciller = Javier Saltares, Jamal Campbell| inker = Jamal Campbell | colorist = | letterer = | editor = | story = | title = [[Prowler (comics)|Prowler]]| volume = 2| issue = 1| date = December 2016| publisher = [[Marvel Comics]]| location = | page = | panel = | id =}}</ref>\n\n==Other versions==\nIn the future timeline of the 1999 \'\'[[Earth X]]\'\' miniseries, Montana had mutated where he developed the ability to change his hands into lassos. He and his fellow Enforcers would be hired as heavies to protect President [[Norman Osborn]]. This protection proved to be ineffectual when the Skull came to New York to take over America. He took control of Montana and the others and then murdered Osborn. Montana\'s fate following the defeat of the Skull remain unrevealed.<ref>\'\'Earth X\'\' #9. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nIn \'\'Spider-Man Noir\'\', Montana and the other Enforcers are muscle for crime boss known as the Goblin.<ref>\'\'Spider-Man Noir\'\' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nThe [[Ultimate Marvel]] incarnation of Montana is \'\'\'Montana Bale\'\'\', an employee of the [[Kingpin (comics)|Kingpin of Crime]] in New York along with [[Fancy Dan]] (Dan Crenshaw) and [[Ox (comics)|Ox]] (Bruno Sanchez).<ref>[[Bendis, Brian Michael]] (w), [[Bagley, Mark]] (p). \'\'Ultimate Spider Man\'\' #10.
